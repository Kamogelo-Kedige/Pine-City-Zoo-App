TIGERS

Wild tigers, the largest members of the big cat family, are now primarily confined to isolated pockets across Asia. Historically, these majestic predators roamed vast regions—from the snowy forests of Siberia and northern China to the tropical jungles of India and Southeast Asia—but widespread human encroachment and environmental changes have drastically limited their natural range.

Today, the tiger is classified as an endangered species, with populations having plummeted by over 90% compared to their historic numbers. Threats such as habitat loss, illegal poaching, and human-wildlife conflict have taken a heavy toll, leaving subspecies like the Bengal, Amur, and Sumatran tigers particularly vulnerable. Robust conservation measures are now critical to preserving these iconic animals and ensuring their survival in the wild.
